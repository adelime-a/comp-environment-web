Corporate Sustainability Policies

Companies' Eco-Friendly Policies

1. Google

From 2010 to 2024, Google signed more than 170 agreements to purchase over 22 GW of clean energy generation, similar to Portugal's total renewable energy in 2024.

In the U.S., Google partnered with Fervo Energy on the world's first corporate agreement for enhanced geothermal power. Geothermal energy is a renewable, 24/7 baseload power and heating source that utilizes the natural thermal heat stored deep beneath the Earth's surface.

Google is investing in long-duration energy storage (LDES) technologies with Energy Dome. LDES is an emerging solution that can store renewable power for longer durations, which helps grids to run on clean energy around the clock.

Google signed the world's first corporate agreement to buy energy from multiple small modular reactors (SMRs) to be developed by Kairos Power. These reactors are designed to provide always-on clean power to U.S. grids by 2035.

2. Microsoft

Microsoft has made significant strides in developing more sustainable data centres. As the world's second largest cloud computing platform, they set ambitious goals to become carbon negative, water positive, and zero waste and to protect more land than they use by 2030.

Microsoft is working toward being carbon negative by 2030 and by 2050 to remove from the atmosphere an equivalent amount of all the carbon dioxide their company has emitted. They have contracted 40 gigawatts (GW) of new renewable energy supply across 26 countries — that's enough energy to power about 10 million US homes.

Microsoft's goal is to be water positive by 2030. They use water to cool their datacenters. To be water positive, Microsoft is innovating on water consumption and management to evaluate how water is used from the physical datacenter buildings all the way to the chip level.

In datacenters, Microsoft targets preventing waste first, then focus on reusing and recovering materials. This includes reusing and recycling construction and demolition waste, diverting operational waste, and advancing circular cloud hardware and packaging.
